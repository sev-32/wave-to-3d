# Phase 4 — Rendering Pipeline & Visual Quality

## The Rendering Challenge

The project must render four distinct visual layers that must look like one cohesive body of water:

1. **Heightfield water surface** — reflections, refractions, Fresnel, caustics
2. **Pool interior** — tiled floor/walls with caustics and underwater tinting
3. **Sphere** — lit by caustics, properly occluded by water surface
4. **Particles** — fluid-like density, not discrete blobs

---

## Layer 1: Water Surface (waterSurfaceVertexShader / FragmentShader)

### Vertex Shader
- Reads heightfield from `tWater` texture
- Displaces Y position by `height` value
- Passes world position, UV, water info, and view direction to fragment

### Fragment Shader (Above view)
- **Refraction**: Bends view ray through water surface using IOR ratio (1.0/1.333)
- **Pool floor sampling**: Traces refracted ray to pool floor, samples tile texture
- **Caustics**: Samples caustics texture at refracted hit point, brightens pool floor
- **Fresnel**: Mix between refracted (pool floor) view and reflected (sky) view based on viewing angle
- **Sphere shadow**: Darkens underwater area near sphere intersection

### Fragment Shader (Below view)
- Same but from underwater perspective (BackSide rendering)

### Key Quality Factors
- Caustics quality depends on `useCaustics` hook — separate render pass
- Fresnel approximation determines how "glassy" vs "mirrorlike" the surface looks
- Normal quality from heightfield gradient affects all lighting

---

## Layer 2: Pool (poolVertexShader / poolFragmentShader)

- Rendered as inside of a box (BackSide culling)
- Receives caustics projection
- Tiled texture with proper UV mapping per face
- Underwater fog/tinting based on depth
- Sphere shadow

## Layer 3: Sphere

- Custom shader with underwater caustics lighting
- Fresnel-like rim lighting
- Underwater portions receive caustic patterns

## Layer 4: Particles (Current State & Roadmap)

### Current: Gaussian Density Splatting
- `THREE.Points` with `gl_PointSize` sizing
- Gaussian alpha falloff per point
- Additive blending for density accumulation
- Very low per-particle alpha (0.04–0.12)

### Problems
1. **No depth compositing with water surface** — particles render independently, don't refract or reflect like the heightfield
2. **No screen-space smoothing** — each particle is a distinct Gaussian blob
3. **No normal reconstruction** — particles don't have proper lighting
4. **Scale calibration** — particle size relative to pool is tricky

### Ideal: Screen-Space Fluid Rendering (from splash reference)

The splash reference implements proper screen-space fluid rendering:

**Pass 1: Depth splatting**
- Render particles to a depth buffer
- Each particle writes its camera-space depth with Gaussian falloff

**Pass 2: Depth smoothing** (bilateral or narrow-range filter)
- Smooth the depth buffer to merge nearby particles
- Preserves sharp edges while filling gaps

**Pass 3: Normal reconstruction**
- Compute screen-space normals from smoothed depth gradients
- `normal = normalize(vec3(dDepth/dx, dDepth/dy, 1.0))`

**Pass 4: Shading & compositing**
- Apply Fresnel, refraction, and lighting using reconstructed normals
- Composite over the scene with proper depth testing

This would make particles look like a continuous fluid surface rather than a point cloud. However, it requires 3-4 additional render passes and render targets.

### Practical Middle Ground
A simpler improvement would be:
1. Keep Gaussian splatting
2. Add a post-process blur pass on particle layer
3. Use depth-aware blending with the water surface
4. Add simple normal estimation from particle depth gradient

## Caustics System (useCaustics hook)

### How Caustics Work
Caustics are the bright patterns on the pool floor caused by light refracting through the water surface. They're generated by:

1. Rendering a plane mesh through the water surface vertex shader
2. Computing how surface normals redirect light
3. Where light rays converge → bright caustics
4. Using `OES_standard_derivatives` (or equivalent) for area-based intensity

### Current Implementation
- Renders to a separate render target texture
- Uses the water heightfield to distort UV coordinates
- Sphere blocks caustics (shadow)

## Visual Quality Priorities (Ordered)

1. **Fix particle scale** — must be tiny relative to pool, accumulate to form fluid mass
2. **Improve emission directionality** — particles should fan out along momentum U, not spray randomly
3. **Add particle-heightfield visual blending** — particles near the surface should merge with it visually
4. **Screen-space depth smoothing** — even a simple blur helps enormously
5. **Foam/spray trails** — age-based visual transition from fluid blob to white spray dots
6. **Normal reconstruction** — for proper lighting on particle fluid masses
